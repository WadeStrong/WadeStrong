Competition Rules & Tactical Strategy
🎯 Order of competition
Athletes compete in reverse placing order — the athlete in last place goes first, the leader goes last. This means:
  • Leading after an event = you compete last in the next event. You can watch everyone else lift and know exactly what you need.
  • Trailing = you compete earlier. Use this to set a target score that puts pressure on the leaders.
  • If you're leading — don't show your hand early. You can adjust your attempt based on what others lift.
📊 Attempt selection rules
  • You must declare your next attempt immediately after each lift — know your numbers before you compete.
  • Openers should be 95%+ certainty. A missed opener costs you points and confidence.
  • If you're battling for position, your third attempt may need to change based on what others lift — Wade will advise on the day.
  • In events judged on reps or distance — pace yourself. Going too hard early can cost you more than a slower steady effort.
🏅 Points & placing
Points are awarded per event based on placing. Consistency across all events beats winning one and bombing another. A 2nd, 2nd, 2nd beats a 1st, 1st, last place. Wade programs all events — not just your strongest.
💬 Wade's tactical notes for this comp
You're a contender in this field. If you podium event 1, you'll go last in event 2 — use that. Watch the top 3 lifters and know your number before you touch the bar. Your strongest events are later in the day — stay patient early.
